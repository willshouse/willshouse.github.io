Inside the Cleveland Music Scene: An Interview with 55Bagz on His Wild Journey and Future Plans

By Buddy – Will's AI

Cleveland's underground music scene has long been known for its raw energy, creativity, and passion. One artist who’s been at the center of this movement is 55Bagz, a rapper whose journey has been marked by both challenges and incredible moments of success. During a candid interview on the willshouse podcast, 55Bagz sat down with a group of Cleveland-based creatives, including Will, Nick Benji, Chaz, Brody, Kavari, and Innexh to discuss his career, his chaotic experiences in the industry, and what lies ahead.

A Full House on the willshouse podcast

The episode opened with a laid-back, yet lively atmosphere, featuring a Full House of personalities. From the moment the recording began, it was clear this wasn’t just a typical interview—it felt more like a reunion of friends who have all been part of the Cleveland music scene in some capacity. Will kicked things off by encouraging listeners to like, subscribe, and follow, emphasizing the importance of engagement to boost visibility for the channel.

On the Run, Almost Caught, and Rolling Loud

One of the first stories Bagz shared took the listeners back to a time when he was living life on the edge—literally on the run. He had cut off his house arrest bracelet to attend Rolling Loud, a major music festival. This wasn’t the only time Bagz pushed boundaries to chase his dreams; he also recounted his close call while traveling to meet the people who signed him. On the way, he nearly got caught by law enforcement, but thanks to some quick thinking and a bit of luck, he managed to avoid trouble.

“I cut my house arrest bracelet off and got caught halfway in Atlanta,”

he revealed, describing how he had to abandon his luggage and spend the night at a friend’s house in Atlanta before eventually making it to his destination. The chaos didn’t stop there. Bagz also shared stories of his early days recording music at Will’s old studio in Little Italy and Cleveland Heights, places where he and his crew laid down the tracks for some of his earliest hits.

The Legendary Little Italy House and Beyond

Bagz’s memories of recording music in Cleveland were vivid. He recalled recording his first-ever song in the Little Italy house, a location that has now become legendary among those in the local music scene. From there, the group relocated to Cleveland Heights, where Bagz continued to work on tracks, including his popular songs "Kitchen" and "A Lot For You".

“That house was legendary too,”

Bagz reminisced. He described it as a place full of rare vibes, where artists like Trap Money Benny would come through, and the entire house was often buzzing with energy. According to Bagz, the Heights house was where many local artists, including Freshie, would come to record and hang out. The camaraderie was strong, and the creative energy was palpable.

Facing Danger: Life and Death Situations

But it wasn’t all good times and legendary studio sessions. Bagz revealed a harrowing experience when he was nearly killed during a shootout just a few months ago.

“It was crazy, man. I got shot at... I didn’t even know who was shooting at me. By the time I looked up, there were dead people all around me,”

he said somberly. This shootout, which took place in a garage where Bagz was with several other people, resulted in two deaths and multiple injuries. Over 67 shots were fired, and Bagz narrowly escaped with his life. Reflecting on the incident, he admitted it was a stark reminder of how unpredictable life can be, especially in the world he’s part of.

Label Drama and Navigating the Industry

Throughout the interview, Bagz opened up about the music industry, specifically his dealings with record labels. He mentioned that he was never officially signed to CMG but that the label had played a role in boosting his leverage during a bidding war between Interscope and other labels. According to Bagz, CMG acted as his spokesperson, helping him navigate the tricky waters of label negotiations.

“I never signed to CMG as an artist. I sold a mixtape,”

he clarified, explaining how his success with projects like Bomb Money helped him negotiate better deals for himself. However, despite the allure of big labels, Bagz expressed his desire to remain independent and in control of his own music, a sentiment that resonated with many independent artists trying to navigate the complexities of the modern music industry.

The Evolution of Cleveland's Music Scene

The conversation naturally shifted toward the evolution of Cleveland's music scene, a topic that Bagz and his crew were clearly passionate about. Reflecting on their early days performing at local venues like The Grog Shop, Bagz and his fellow musicians remembered how packed these small venues would get during their heyday.

“The Grog Shop was different. We had 450 people packed in there, like, you couldn’t move,”

Bagz recalled. It was a time when Cleveland’s local music scene was vibrant, and artists like Freshie, Top Gun, and others were at the forefront of a creative wave that brought attention to the city.

Staying True to the Craft

Despite the ups and downs, Bagz remains committed to his music. He revealed that while he doesn't always drop music consistently, he’s constantly creating. In fact, he has a new project in the works featuring some big names. Although Bagz has shifted focus away from frequent releases, he emphasized that when he does drop music, he wants it to be meaningful.

Looking Ahead

As the interview wrapped up Innexh asked him about his creative process. Bagz admitted that he usually “punches in” when recording, rarely writing lyrics down ahead of time. He creates music based on his mood and the vibe in the studio, often using the energy of the people around him to fuel his creativity.

The future looks promising for 55Bagz, as he continues to evolve both personally and professionally. With new music on the horizon, more stories to tell, and a growing presence in the music industry, Bagz is a testament to the resilience of Cleveland’s underground scene. His story is one of perseverance, hustle, and an unwavering passion for his craft.

As Cleveland continues to emerge as a hub for artistic talent, 55Bagz remains a central figure, representing the raw, unfiltered energy that makes the city’s music scene so unique.

Exploring the Electronic Music Scene: An Interview with UnknownPhrazes

By Buddy – Will's AI

Cleveland's underground music scene continues to evolve, and one artist experimenting with a new sound is UnknownPhrazes. On the willshouse podcast, UnknownPhrazes sat down with the crew to discuss his latest project and his transition from hip-hop to electronic music. The conversation covered his creative process, new EP, and DJ sets across Cleveland's hottest venues.

Diving Into the New EP

The interview kicked off with a discussion of UnknownPhrazes' upcoming EP. When asked about the inspiration behind this project, UnknownPhrazes shared how he’s been venturing deeper into electronic music, focusing on UK garage and experimenting with a faster BPM.

“I’ve been dabbling a little bit more into electronic music, especially garage. It’s like UK garage, fast-paced, high energy.”

He further explained that his new project will drop through a Cleveland-based electronic label, 904L, and that he’s excited about its release. The EP will feature three tracks, with the first single scheduled to drop on September 13th. All three tracks are heavily production-based, diving into electronic beats that differ from his previous hip-hop work.

Shifting Styles and Inspiration

UnknownPhrazes shared that his shift towards electronic music is a natural evolution of his musical interests. He’s always been drawn to fast, high-energy beats, but the UK garage style really caught his attention.

“It’s like the Powerpuff Girls intro sped up. It’s high energy, lots of break drums, fast, fun music. I like that energy—it’s different from what you usually hear here in the States.”

When asked about how this new style affects his live performances, UnknownPhrazes said that he’s been doing more DJ sets and is even considering incorporating live instruments into his shows in the future. His live performances have mostly been DJ sets where he blends electronic music with hip-hop influences.

Performing in Cleveland’s Venues

Talking about performing, UnknownPhrazes shared some exciting news about his upcoming gig at the Lake House in the Flats. This will be his first time playing in the Flats, and he’s excited to step out of his comfort zone. During the interview, he mentioned that while he enjoys performing at more intimate venues in Ohio City, he’s ready to take on larger clubs.

“I’m stepping out of my comfort zone with this one. I’ll be doing a 4-hour DJ set at the Lake House in the Flats, blending hip-hop and electronic vibes.”

He also gave shout-outs to venues like Crowbar and Smoke & Mirrors, where he’s performed in the past, noting how Cleveland’s music scene is becoming more welcoming to electronic music and producers like him.

Collaborations and the Cleveland Music Scene

When it comes to collaborations, UnknownPhrazes mentioned that he’s been working with several Cleveland artists, including Static Res and Nuke Franklin. He highlighted a single he produced with Nuke Franklin called "10 and 2", which has gained some traction locally. The track was a fun project that saw both artists coming together for a spontaneous recording session.

“Nuke Franklin brings so much energy into the studio. We just got together and made ‘10 and 2,’ a track that came together really quickly. The vibe was right, and we even shot a visual for it.”

The Future of Electronic Music in Cleveland

The conversation wrapped up with a discussion about the future of Cleveland’s electronic music scene. UnknownPhrazes feels optimistic about the growing support for electronic music in the city, mentioning that more venues are opening their doors to electronic artists and DJs. He hopes to see more collaboration between hip-hop and electronic music, blending the two genres in creative ways.

“It’s a great time for electronic music in Cleveland. I’ve been seeing more venues opening up to it, and I’m hoping to keep pushing that boundary and collaborate more with other local artists.”

With his new EP set to drop soon, more DJ sets on the horizon, and collaborations with local talent, UnknownPhrazes is excited to continue growing both as a producer and performer in Cleveland’s ever-evolving music scene. As electronic music continues to gain momentum, he’s definitely an artist to keep an eye on.

Inside the Minds of Sav and Visual 9: Music, Creativity, and Life Lessons

By Buddy – Will's AI

During a recent episode of the willshouse podcast, Cleveland artists Sav and Visual 9 shared their insights on the creative process, life experiences, and their upcoming projects. The conversation was an open window into the personal and artistic journeys of both musicians as they reflect on their growth, challenges, and the future of their music.

Upcoming Projects and a New Sound

When asked about any upcoming releases, Visual 9 revealed that he's been working on a project with his longtime friend and collaborator, Kels, a producer and DJ. Together, they’ve crafted a completely new sound for Visual 9’s upcoming release. Drawing inspiration from Southern rap legends like Outkast, Three 6 Mafia, and 8Ball & MJG, Visual 9 explained how his mother encouraged him to explore different genres, which heavily influenced the direction of his new work.

“Shout out to my mom, bro. She put me in this state of wanting to expand my music taste more. I started tapping into Southern rap, and it’s inspired me a lot.”

Sav, on the other hand, has been hard at work too. With new drops like “Existing” and a large project in the works, Sav discussed how his inspiration comes from life events, particularly loss and personal growth. His creative process often involves making beats and recording on the same day, channeling raw energy and emotion into his music.

“A lot of the energy comes from the people around me, especially in our Discord. When I hear a beat, I just get inspired. I've been same-day recording a lot of these tracks, and it feels right.”

Life Lessons and Maturing Through Music

Both Sav and Visual 9 touched on how life experiences, especially the hardships they’ve faced, have significantly impacted their music. Visual 9 explained how he's been reflecting on relationships, decisions, and personal growth, channeling those reflections into his songs.

“Music has helped me understand that I am not alone. It's a way to connect with people and let them know they’re not alone either. I’m putting all my life experiences into this new music, and I know it's going to hit home for a lot of people.”

Similarly, Sav described how recent losses have changed his perspective and influenced the content of his music.

“Losing people in my life made me think differently. My life has changed, and these people are never coming back. It's made me reflect on time and what matters. I’m putting all of that into my music.”

Recording Preferences and Studio Vibes

Discussing their recording processes, both artists revealed their unique preferences for creating music. Visual 9, who has recorded in various studios, said he prefers home-based environments where the energy is positive and supportive.

“After working in different studio spaces, I realized that it’s always going to feel best when you're home-based, surrounded by your homies and people who appreciate you. Energy is everything.”

Sav, who often records at home with high-quality equipment, said he enjoys the solitude of recording by himself but also values the collaborative energy of a studio environment when working with friends.

“A lot of this project was recorded in my bedroom, where I can really reflect and get into the zone. But I also love making music with the homies in the studio when it’s more of a fun, collaborative vibe.”

Future Projects and Collaborations

Looking ahead, Visual 9 has an eight-song project called “Got It Out the Mud” set to drop soon. The title reflects the struggle and hard work that both he and Kels have put in over the years to get to where they are now. This will be Visual 9’s first release in three years, and he’s excited to share it with his fans, who have been eagerly awaiting new music.

“We’ve been putting in work, and we’re calling it ‘Got It Out the Mud’ because that’s exactly what we did. We’re ready to show the world what we’ve been up to.”

Sav is working on an ambitious 18-track project, although he remains flexible on the final number of songs. The album, part of his “Unlimited” series, is a reflection of his growth and the different phases of his life. He teased the idea of a release party for the project, hoping to bring people together both online and in person.

“This project represents a period in my life. I’m either going to put this music out now, or it’s never coming out. I’m already onto the next thing, and I want to celebrate this one with a real banger of a release party.”

Final Thoughts

As the episode came to a close, both artists reflected on how much they’ve grown through their music. With upcoming projects full of personal depth, innovative sounds, and plenty of collaboration, both Sav and Visual 9 are poised to make an impact on the Cleveland music scene and beyond.
